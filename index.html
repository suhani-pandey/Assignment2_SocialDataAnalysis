Takeaway

The city doesn't
just change at night.
It transforms by the hour.

After exploring 22 years of crime data, one thing is clear: San Francisco is not the same city at every hour. Crime doesn’t just rise and fall—it shifts, transforms, and follows its own hidden schedule. Each type of crime has its own “rush hour,” and the city’s risks change dramatically between weekday afternoons and the late hours of the weekend. What feels safe at noon might be unpredictable at 2AM.

Understanding these rhythms helps us see the city not as a single story, but as a living pattern—one where time, day, and social life all shape what happens next. The 9AM city and the 2AM city share the same streets. But they're not the same place.

Data & Methodology

Incident data spans 2003–2025 from SFPD official records, standardized and cleaned. All averages are per-day means within each day-type (weekday or weekend). Crime categories are standardized across reporting periods.
